Bridgeport History

 The city of Bridgeport is located in Fairfield County in the southern part of the state on the Long Island Sound. It is Connecticutâ€™s most populous city. Settled in the mid-17th century as part of the township of Stratford, Bridgeport incorporated as a town in 1821 and a city in 1836. While early settlers farmed and fished, they soon took advantage of their location on Black Rock Harbor and shifted from an agrarian community to a mercantile and manufacturing hub. When the railroad opened in 1840, the city became a booming industrial center and produced everything from sewing machines to ammunition.
